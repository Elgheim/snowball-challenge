DNB ASA

About:

DNB ASA (OSE: DNB) is Norway's largest financial group, with equity of approximately NOK 250 billion as of June 2019. The group was founded on 4 December 2003 following a merger between Den norske Bank (DnB) and Gjensidige NOR. The company's largest shareholder is the Norwegian state at the Ministry of Trade and Industry (34 per cent), the second largest being the Sparebankstiftelsen DNB (approx. 8 per cent). The company changed its name in November 2011 to DNB. DNB is the Nordic region's second largest financial group after the Swedish bank Nordea. DNB manages a capital of NOK 2,898 billion, and has total lending of NOK 1,535 billion to customers as of 31 March 2016. The group has its head office and business address in Bjørvika in Oslo.
